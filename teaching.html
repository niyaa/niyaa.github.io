My primary goal is to empower students with the knowledge and skills necessary to solve complex engineering problems efficiently and effectively. Computational Mechanics is the backbone of modern engineering, combining principles from mechanics, mathematics, and computer science to model, simulate, and analyze complex engineering systems. In my teaching, I aim to create an engaging learning environment that cultivates critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and a deep understanding of fundamental concepts. I have created three subjects namely "Automation of Process using Python", "Advanced Computational Fluid Mechanics" and "Hydrodynamics instabilites". All theses subjects material is open source and available on my Github. Apart from these i taught subjects such as "Computational Fluid Mechanics", "Advanced Computational Fluid Mechanics", "Fluid Mechanics", "Numerical Methods 1", "Numerical Methods 2" and "Finite Element Methods".
